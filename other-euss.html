Check if you need a UK visa: Information based on your answers

You need a visa to join your family member in the UK

Based on your answers, you might be eligible for 6 visas.

EU Settlement Scheme [only show to EEA/Swiss users] If your family member is from the EEA or Switzerland and you have a biometric passport or ID card

You may be able to join your close family member by applying to the free EU Settlement Scheme.

Close family members you can join are your:

  • parent, grandparent or great-grandparent - if you're over 21 you must be dependent on them
  • child, grandchild or great-grandchild, if you're dependent on them

You can also join the close family members of your spouse or civil partner.

Can work and study

Yes

You can study and work in any job

Need a minimum income

No

There are no financial requirements

Need knowledge of English

No


Initial visa length

5 years

If you successfully apply, you'll get 'pre-settled status' in live in the UK for 5 years. 

Can extend

Not applicable

Instead of extending, you can apply to settle (called 'settled status') after 5 years

Can settle

Yes - after 5 years

You can apply for 'settled status' to live permanently in the UK. It's free to apply. If you successfully apply, you can use this status to apply for citizenship

Find out more about the EU Settlement Scheme

EU Settlement Scheme [only show to non-EEA/Swiss users]If your family member is from the EEA or Switzerland and you have an eligible UK residence document

You may be able to join your close family member by applying to the free EU Settlement Scheme.

Close family members you can join are your:

  • parent, grandparent or great-grandparent - if you're over 21 you must be dependent on them
  • child, grandchild or great-grandchild, if you're dependent on them

You can also join the close family members of your spouse or civil partner.

You must have a UK biometric residence card that has not expired.

Can work and study

Yes

You can study and work in any job

Need a minimum income

No

There are no financial requirements

Need knowledge of English

No

Initial visa length

5 years

If you successfully apply, you'll get 'pre-settled status' in live in the UK for 5 years. 

Can extend

Not applicable

Instead of extending, you can apply to settle (called 'settled status') after 5 years

Can settle

Yes - after 5 years

You can apply for 'settled status' to live permanently in the UK. It's free to apply. If you successfully apply, you can use this status to apply for citizenship

EU Settlement Scheme family permitIf your family member is from the EEA or Switzerland

You may be able to join your close family member by applying to the free EU Settlement Scheme.

Close family members you can join are your:

  • parent, grandparent or great-grandparent - if you're over 21 you must be dependent on them
  • child, grandchild or great-grandchild, if you're dependent on them
You can also join the close family members of your spouse or civil partner.

Can work and study

Yes

You can study and work in any job

Need a minimum income

No

There are no financial requirements

Need knowledge of English

No

Initial visa length

4 or 6 months

Can extend

Yes

You can apply to the EU Settlement Scheme for 'pre-settled status' to live in the UK for 5 years. It's free to apply. You must usually apply within 3 months of arriving in the UK

Can settle

Yes - after 5 years

You can apply for 'settled status' to live permanently in the UK. It's free to apply. You can use this status to apply for citizenship

Find out more about the EU Settlement Scheme family permit

Parent visa If your child has permission to live permanently in the UK

You may be able to join your child if they're a British or Irish citizen, are settled in the UK, or have 'pre-settled status'.

Can work and study

Yes

You can study and work in any job

Need a minimum income

No

There's no minimum income requirement but you'll need to support yourself without relying on certain benefits

Need knowledge of English

Yes - with exceptions

You must usually prove you can speak and understand English. If you do not, you still may be eligible in exceptional circumstances

Initial visa length

2 years and 9 months

Can extend

Yes

You can extend as many times as you want, as long as you're eligible

Can settle

Yes - usually after 5 years

Settling means you can live permanently in the UK. If you successfully apply, you can use this status to apply for citizenship

Find out more about the parent visa

Adult dependant relative visa If you'll be cared for by an eligible family member with permission to live permanently in the UK

You may be able to join your eligible family member if they're a British or Irish citizen, are settled in the UK, or have 'pre-settled status'.

The eligibility criteria include:

  • your relative being your parent, grandchild, brother, sister, son or daughter
  • you needing long-term care to do everyday tasks because of illness, disability or age
  • the care you need not being available or affordable in the country you live in
  • your relative being able to support, accommodate and care for you

Can work and study

Yes

You can study and work in any job

Need a minimum income

No

There are no financial requirements

Need knowledge of English

No

Initial visa length

2 years and 9 months

Your stay is unlimited if your family member is British, Irish or settled. Otherwise, your visa will end on the same date as your family member

Can extend

Yes

You only need to extend if your family member is not British, Irish or settled

Can settle

Yes

You only need to apply to settle if your family member is not British, Irish or settled. Settling means you can live permanently in the UK. If you successfully apply, you can use this status to apply for citizenship

Find out more about the adult dependant relative visa

Parent of a Child Student visa If your child is studying at a UK independent school

You can join your child if they have or are applying for a Child Student visa to attend an independent school in the UK.

The eligibility criteria include:

  • your child being aged between 4 and 11
  • you being the only parent accompanying your child
  • you maintaining your main home outside the UK

Can work and study

No

Need a minimum income

No

There's no minimum income requirement but you need savings to support you and your child

Need knowledge of English

No

Initial visa length

Depends on child

Your visa will end on the same date as your child's

Can extend

Yes

You can extend as long as your child is under 12 and you're still eligible

Can settle

No

Settling means you can live permanently in the UK. If you successfully apply, you can use this status to apply for citizenship

Find out more about the Parent of a Child Student visa

Other ways to join your partner or family
There may be other ways to join your partner or family. For example, if you come to the UK:

  • for work - it can be cheaper or faster to apply in some situations
  • for tourism or shorter family visits - it can be suitable for stays of less than 6 months
